Prescriptions & Medications

How a Medication Tracker Keeps Doses on Schedule

A medication tracker's whole job is to remove "did I take that?" from your day. MediNest's medication tracker logs dosage, frequency, and which days a medicine is due — then reminds you at the right time, for the right person, without you having to remember anything yourself.

What a medication tracker should actually track

  • •Dosage and frequency. How much, how often, and on which specific days.
  • •Who prescribed it. The doctor's name attached, for quick reference at refill time.
  • •Adherence history. A record of which doses were actually taken on schedule.
  • •Per-person tracking. Every family member's medications tracked separately, not merged into one list.

Setting up a medication in MediNest

  1. Open the family member's profile and go to Medications.
  2. Add the medication name, dosage, and prescribing doctor.
  3. Choose the frequency — daily or specific days of the week.
  4. Turn on reminders to get notified at dose time.

Frequently asked questions

How does a medication tracker know when to remind me?

You set the frequency (daily, specific days) and time for each medication once. MediNest then reminds you automatically for every dose, and separately when a refill is due, based on how much is left.

Can a medication tracker handle different schedules for different family members?

Yes. In MediNest, each family member's medications and schedules are tracked independently, so a child's twice-daily antibiotic and a parent's weekday-only prescription don't get mixed up.
